From stone & metal, to clay tablets, to papyrus, to animal skin, to birch bark, to palm leaf, to paper, this is how our writing materials evolved. But did it stop here? No, it again evolved and now we also write or type on tablets/ laptops/desktops.
From using plants as clothes, to using animal skin, to weaving and spinning plant and animal fibers, to using manmade and synthetic fibers, this is how our clothing evolved.
From using branches and saplings, to building huts with thick logs, to using stones, to building shelters with various kinds of other synthetic materials, this is how our shelters evolved.
From eating plants, to eating raw animals, to learning to heat and cook on fire, to adding flavors and enhancing it according to our taste buds, this is how our food evolved.
From smoke signals, to making sounds, to using gestures, to using words, to messenger pigeons, to telephones and emails, to video calls, this is how our communication has evolved.
From walking long distances, to travelling on animals, to inventing wheels, to using land and water ways for travelling, to using air planes, this is how our transportation evolved.
Anything and everything has evolved. Of course, it is for a better good, bringing along its set of cons.
